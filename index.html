Rodolfo (Rudy) Corona

I currently have the privilege of working with Dr. Zeynep Akata at the University of Amsterdam, supported by a Fulbright Student Research grant, and in collaboration with Dr. Trevor Darrell at UC Berkeley. I received my bachelor's degree in computer science from UT Austin, in the Turing Scholars honors program, and was fortunate to be working with Drs. Raymond Mooney and Qixing Huang at the intersection of natural language processing and computer graphics. In August of 2019 I will start my PhD at UC Berkeley.

Through my research, I hope to contribute to the understanding of how intelligent systems form representations of meaning. Particularly, I am interested in domains involving perception and language grounding. I am also generally interested in interpretability within machine learning.
