Rodolfo (Rudy) Corona

I am a second-year PhD student at UC Berkeley in BAIR co-advised by Trevor Darrell and Dan Klein.

Prior to this, I spent a year working with Zeynep Akata in the Amsterdam Machine Learning Lab through the support of a Fulbright Student Research. I received my bachelor's degree in computer science from UT Austin, and was fortunate to get introduced to research by Ray Mooney and Qixing Huang.

Through my research, I hope to contribute to the understanding of how intelligent systems form representations of meaning within the context of multisensory perception and communication. I am particularly interested in language grounding, embodied cognition, and methods for bridging the gap between the processing of raw sensory information and symbolic reasoning. I am also generally interested in interpretability within machine learning and communication in multiagent systems.

I am currently supported by a Ford Foundation fellowship, and am an NSF GRFP fellow. Prior to this I was funded by a Berkeley Chancellor's fellowship.
