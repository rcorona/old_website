Rodolfo (Rudy) Corona

I am a second-year PhD student at UC Berkeley in BAIR co-advised by Trevor Darrell and Dan Klein.

Through my research, I hope to contribute to the understanding of how intelligent systems form concepts and abstractions necessary for high-level reasoning, starting from sensorimotor input. Work in compositionality, abstraction, and embodied cognition is of great interest to me. I am also fascinated by the notion of operationalizing and self-reflection and self-awareness in machines, and am motivated by the thought that studying AI is an introspective exercise in itself :)

I am also passionate about contributing to efforts related to diversity, equity, and inclusion. Currently, I am a co-organizer of RIIAA, an officer in Bias Busters, and a coordinator in the BAIR mentoring program.

Prior to this, I spent a year working with Zeynep Akata in the Amsterdam Machine Learning Lab through the support of a Fulbright Student Research grant. I received my bachelor's degree in computer science from UT Austin, and was fortunate to get introduced to research by Ray Mooney and Qixing Huang.

I am currently supported by a Ford Foundation fellowship and am an NSF GRFP fellow. Prior to this, I was funded by a Berkeley Chancellor's fellowship.
