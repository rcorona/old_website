Rodolfo (Rudy) Corona

I am an incoming PhD student at UC Berkeley in BAIR co-advised by Trevor Darrell and Dan Klein. Prior to this, I completed a Fulbright Student Research grant working with Zeynep Akata in the Amsterdam Machine Learning Lab.

I received my bachelor's degree in computer science from UT Austin, in the Turing Scholars honors program, and was fortunate to get introduced to research by Ray Mooney and Qixing Huang.

Through my research, I hope to contribute to the understanding of how intelligent systems form representations of meaning within the context of multisensory perception and communication. I am particularly interested in language grounding, embodied cognition, and methods for bridging the gap between the processing of raw sensory information and symbolic reasoning. I am also generally interested in interpretability within machine learning and communication in multiagent systems.
